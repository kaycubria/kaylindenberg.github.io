Analyzing a Past Conflict and My Responses to It

Friday, January 23, 2015

I am extremely conflict adverse.

My typical response to a potential conflict is to avoid it. This is usually practiced by letting the other party do whatever they want to and then dealing with the consequences. I am also a big believer in allowing people to be autonomous individuals, so I think stepping back and letting people do what they want to comes fairly naturally.

Unfortunately there are times when conflict is unavoidable.

An old colleague of mine who was extremely conflict-oriented used to like to ask my opinion just so she could contradict what I said. Typically, I would just ignore her provocation by saying something along the lines of “that’s an interesting way to look at things” or “I hadn’t thought of it that way before.” I did feel annoyed, but I also respected that I was in a workplace and needed to be able to continue to work with my colleague, even if we didn’t agree on everything (or anything, apparently).

Now that I no longer work with that colleague, I sometimes think I should have spoken up and let her know that I didn’t appreciate being constantly contradicted and criticized. I think doing so in a polite manner would have at least allowed me to feel like I had tried to stand up for myself and improve an unpleasant situation. I’m not sure it would have fixed anything, but it probably couldn’t have made things worse.
